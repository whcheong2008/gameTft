The overarching theme is about 2 best friends, one (A) who believes in deontology, the other (B) believes in utilitarianism. Their differences are actually only exposed when facing extreme situations. Otherwise its generally not shown. The actual main character is A. They face differences in opinions along the way on their journey but the matters are easily resolved up, their relationship slowly gets challenged, all these must happen subtly until a critical point somewhere in the middle (region 4).
In region 4, a discovery regarding their fundamental understanding of how the way the world works is uprooted and this results in a catastrophically challenging decision. They have to sacrifice a group that's very close to them (non-combatants in their group?) or risk world destruction, A refuses to accept. A and B fights (could be one of the mission, the group splits), but a boss arrives then they band together to fight. The end is that B sacrifices his life to lengthen the time that A has before world destruction, attempting to believe in A's ideology. Instead of world destruction, a few villages gets destroyed. (The villages that destroyed needs to have people the group met, interacted with along the way so that it doesnt feel like A's choice is the better one). As a result, the group split.

Region 5 is about gathering the group back.

The group reunites even though their ideology is split and due to B's death. Because they have to complete the journey and stop the end of the world.
