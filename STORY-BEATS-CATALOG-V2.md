## STAGE STRUCTURE REFERENCE
From STORY-STAGES.md:
- **Story Stages** (2-3 per region): Heavy narrative weight, cutscenes/extended dialogue, major plot advancement
- **Character Stages** (2-3 per region): Medium weight, brief dialogue + combat dialogue, relationship beats
- **Gameplay Stages** (2-3 per region): Light weight, brief card + combat dialogue, mechanic-focused
- **Boss Stages** (1 per region): Heavy weight, pre-fight scene + combat events + post-fight scene

**Storytelling Tools:**
- Full cutscene (major moments only, 6-8 total)
- Dialogue scene (2-4 per region)
- Combat dialogue (every stage can have some)
- Environmental storytelling (every stage)
- Brief card (as needed)
- Flashback overlay (1-2 total, R8)

# CHAPTER 1 — THE FRONTIER
**Region: 1 (Eastern Frontier)**
**Estimated Stages: 1-1 through 1-8**

## Beat 1-1: The Approach & Settlement Introduction
- **What happens:** Kael, Lyric, Senna, and Otho arrive at a frontier settlement under Voidspawn threat. They assess the palisade breaches and plan their defensive strategy.
- **Characters involved:** Kael (leader), Lyric (diplomat), Senna (Veilborn attunist), Otho (scholar), Settlement militia captain Torren
- **Type:** Dialogue/Environmental description
- **Location in chapter:** Beginning (opening scene)
- **Stage mapping:** Story Stage (1-1) — Opening dialogue scene, introduces core mechanic (Veilborn attunement), sets frontier tone
- **Cutscene candidate:** NO — use dialogue scene with character introductions
- **Combat dialogue:** Minimal
- **Environmental storytelling:** Settlement palisade deterioration, frontier isolation, "Veil's breath" on horizon

## Beat 4-5: Otho's Revelation
- **What happens:** Otho presents findings in observatory main chamber. Uses ancient scholar data (birth registries of Veilborn, Veil-crossing event logs). Ancient scholars used one word for Echoes AND Voidspawn together: "Veilcrossers." We assumed they hadn't separated the two planes. They hadn't because there was nothing to separate. Birth registry data maps exactly to crossing logs. Every surge in Veilborn population = both Echo and Voidspawn increases. Every population decline = both decrease. There's no Luminous Plane. No Void. One Otherside. Veilborn are the ONLY opening. Echoes and Voidspawn are same entity type. Corruption comes from crossing energy without guidance. Water harmless in stream, destructive in flood. The Sovereign isn't ancient evil pressing inward — it's accumulation of corruption over centuries. Eruption here: Veil breaking globally. Killing every Veilborn would seal the Veil permanently. Sovereign, cut off, dies.
- **Characters involved:** Otho (speaker), Senna, Lyric, Kael, Ren, Sera, Maren, Mira
- **Type:** Exposition / Dialogue scene
- **Location in chapter:** Middle
- **Stage mapping:** Story Stage (4-6) — CRITICAL REVELATION, tonal shift, philosophical catastrophe

- **Environmental storytelling:** Observatory interior, ancient records

## Beat 4-8: Lyric's Morning & Their Fight
- **What happens:** Morning. Lyric walks toward Veilborn tent openly, hand on weapon. Kael steps into path. Everything's been said. What's left is simpler and worse. They fight. Echoes clashing, energy rippling. Lyric smart, aggressive, adaptive — uses tactics they developed together, knows Kael's tendencies. But trying to get *past* Kael, not kill him. Aiming for the tent beyond. Ren holds flank without being asked. Sera stands apart, unwilling to fight either side.
