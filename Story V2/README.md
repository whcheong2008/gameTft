# Story V2 — README

## What This Is

The novelized story of "The Shattered Veil" — an auto-battler game with 8 regions, 74 stages, and a central moral dilemma about whether it's right to sacrifice a few to save everyone.

These 8 chapter files read as a continuous narrative — cover to cover, no game knowledge required. A reader picking this up cold should be able to follow the entire story.

## The Story in Brief

Two Resonants (superhumanly enhanced warriors who channel elemental spirits called Echoes) and their Veilborn companions (non-combatants who sustain the Echoes) discover that the Veilborn are the source of both the helpful Echoes AND the destructive Voidspawn. The best friend (Lyric) argues for killing all Veilborn to save the world. The protagonist (Kael) refuses. Lyric dies buying time. Kael finds an alternative — a seal that would close the Veil without killing anyone. At the end, the seal needs more energy than expected. Two Veilborn choose to sacrifice themselves. The third — a sixteen-year-old girl — is still deciding when Kael, in a moment of panic as the seal fails, forces the energy through her without consent. The world is saved. Kael broke his own principles.

## Key Characters

**Resonants (combat heroes):**
- **Kael** — Protagonist. Principled. Won't sacrifice the people who trust him. Breaks his own principle at the end.
- **Lyric** — Best friend. Utilitarian. Argues for killing Veilborn to save the world. Dies in R4 buying time.
- **Ren** — Tank. Barely speaks. Communicates through positioning. Loyal to Kael since training.
- **Sera** — Mage. Competitive, pragmatic. Leaves in R4, returns humbled in R5.
- **Maren** — Healer. Empathetic. Understands both sides. Leaves in R4, returns first in R5.
- **Voss** — Joins R7. Blunt, practical. Treats Veilborn as functional infrastructure, not a moral dilemma.

**Veilborn (non-combatant):**
- **Senna** — Primary attunist. Maternal. Reads emotional states through bonds. Chooses to burn in R8.
- **Otho** — Scholar. Carries the data that breaks the world open. Chooses to burn in R8.
- **Mira** — 16. Sharp, growing. Keeps a notebook. Doesn't get to finish her last sentence.

## Writing Rules Established During Development

These came from iterative review. Follow them if editing or extending:

1. **No telegraphing.** Never write "this was the last time" or "not yet" or "he let it go" — anything that tells the reader a clue matters or a pattern will escalate.
2. **No narrator explaining character traits.** Don't write "That was Lyric's gift: he could make anyone feel like they mattered." Show it through action.
3. **No counting patterns.** Don't write "the second disagreement" or "first time in three years." Let events happen without the narrator tracking them.
4. **No flagging language.** Avoid "standard explanation," "everyone knows that" (followed by silence), "he let it go." These all scream "pay attention here."
5. **Trust the reader.** If a clue is well-placed, the reader doesn't need the narrator to linger on it. End the scene and move on.
6. **Natural hype is fine.** Lines like "He wasn't" or "the last time everything would feel simple" are okay — they build tension without pointing at anything specific.
7. **Show Kael's character through action.** He doesn't just say "their choice" about the family — he reinforces their farmhouse and leaves them weapons.
8. **The seal scene uses visual descriptions, not percentages.** Tears shrinking, sky closing, ground reverting from crystal to stone. No "80%... 95%."
9. **Mira scenes work even if she survived.** None exist solely to make her death sadder. She's a person developing, not a tragedy prop.

## Lore Quick Reference

- **The Veil** — Barrier between mortal realm and the Otherside.
- **False understanding** — Two separate dimensions (Luminous Plane = good Echoes, Void = bad Voidspawn). Two doors.
- **Truth** — One Otherside. One door. Veilborn ARE the door. Echoes and Voidspawn come through the same opening.
- **The Sovereign** — Not a villain. A weather system. Accumulated corruption that grows while the Veil is open.
- **Resonants** — Born with outward channeling. Enhanced physicality (permanent). Amplify Echoes through their bodies.
- **Veilborn** — Born with inward sensitivity. Sustain Echoes, maintain bonds. No combat enhancement. The two traits are mutually exclusive.
- **Veil Essence** — Game currency. Harvested from defeated Voidspawn. Funds summoning, upgrades, everything.
- **Echoes** — Elemental spirits from the Otherside. Stored in Veil Crystals (roster). Sustained by Veilborn (team). Channeled by Resonants (combat).

## Plot Patches (Potential Plot Holes Addressed)

Six pressure points were identified and patched:

1. **Lyric's seal vs R8 finale** — Ch4 clarifies Resonants can patch tears crudely but can't reshape the Veil. Only Veilborn can do structural sealing.
2. **Mira's timing** — Ch8 makes urgency unmistakable: sealed tears are cracking back open, Sovereign reforming, seconds from total reversal.
3. **Why only three Veilborn** — Ch5: Ren asks. Otho answers: traveling Veilborn thin the Veil along the route, a convoy would leave destruction behind it.
4. **Burn mechanic arrives earlier** — Ch6: Otho explicitly describes burning as a known last resort, "not survivable." Senna hears it.
5. **Wider world reaction** — Ch5: military command fractured, communications fragmented, no coordinated response because the truth split the command structure.
6. **Resonant baseline surviving the seal** — Ch1: Kael notices his permanent physical enhancement before channeling, establishes it's separate from the Veil connection.
