# Chapter 7 — The Proving Grounds

They found Voss fighting a Voidspawn patrol alone in a canyon where reality stuttered like a skipping record.

He was good. Even Kael could see that immediately — the man moved with the economy of someone who'd been fighting without support for so long that every motion was optimized for efficiency. No wasted energy. No hesitation. No fear, not because he was brave, but because fear was a luxury he'd trimmed away months ago.

His Echoes were the problem. The bonds were degrading — visible even from a distance. The spirits flickered, their forms incomplete, their attacks landing at half-power. A Resonant's attunement decayed without a Veilborn to maintain it, and this man had been running without one for a long time.

Kael's group intervened. Ren crashed into the Voidspawn flank. Sera vaporized the largest creature with a burst of concentrated magic. The patrol scattered and dissolved.

The man — Voss — stood in the aftermath, breathing hard. He looked at the group. Counted them. Assessed. His eyes landed on Senna and Mira and stayed there.

"Veilborn," he said.

"Two of them," Kael confirmed. "Three, counting Otho."

"I need attunement. My bonds are degrading. I'll be combat-ineffective in two weeks." He said it without emotion — a status report. "I heard about your seal plan on the eastern channels. I came to find you."

"Why?"

"Because you have Veilborn and I need them. And because you're heading to the Wellspring and you need another fighter." He shrugged. "Simple math."

---

Senna refreshed his attunement that evening. It took longer than usual — the degradation was severe.

"How long have you been running without a Veilborn?" she asked, her fingers working through the corroded bonds.

"Four months."

She stopped. Looked at him. "That shouldn't be possible."

"I'm stubborn."

"Where's your attunist?"

"Left. After the truth spread. She couldn't handle knowing what she was." A pause. "I don't blame her. But I needed to keep fighting."

Senna returned to the attunement. She worked in silence for a while.

"Your bonds are badly corroded. I can restore them, but it'll take a few days of regular sessions."

"Fine." He didn't thank her. He didn't need to. The transaction was clear.

---

Voss integrated quickly because he didn't try to integrate at all. He didn't seek friendship, didn't contribute to camp conversation, didn't ask about the group's history or their feelings about the mission. He trained, he fought, he ate, he slept.

What he did do — and this was the thing that mattered — was treat the Veilborn like professionals.

When he needed an attunement adjustment, he asked Mira. "Your secondary bonds are pulling. Can you tighten them?" She did. He thanked her. He moved on. No guilt in the interaction. No avoidance. No philosophical weight. Just a competent person asking another competent person for a service.

Mira had spent months being looked at with complicated emotions — pity from Maren, guilt from Kael, careful avoidance from Sera's returned squad members. Voss looked at her and saw someone useful. Essential, even. The infrastructure that kept him fighting.

She started sitting closer to the group at meals. Not because the tension was gone — the others still carried their baggage around the Veilborn — but because Voss's blunt practicality reminded her that she had value beyond being a problem to solve.

---

The Proving Grounds were a warzone. The Sovereign's forces were concentrated here — as if the entity, on some instinctive level, sensed that the group was heading for the Wellspring and was trying to block their path. The terrain warped constantly. Gravity shifted for seconds at a time. The sky flickered between worlds so rapidly that it strobed. Voidspawn materialized from the ground mid-step, emerging from tears that opened and closed in heartbeats.

The group fought through it day after day. Five Resonants now — Kael, Ren, Sera, Maren, and Voss. Three Veilborn sustaining them. The combat was relentless. Every mile east was earned in blood and Veil Essence.

On the fourth day, during a coordinated assault on a Voidspawn stronghold, Kael ordered the team to push through a chokepoint — a narrow canyon where the terrain funneled the Voidspawn into a concentrated area. It made sense tactically. More concentrated enemies meant higher burst damage efficiency.

Ren held the line easily, his shield absorbing the creatures' charge. But something was wrong with the angle. The enemies hitting from above the canyon rim were getting clean shots down on Maren and Mira. The position was good for offense but weak defensively.

Ren looked at Kael, then at the canyon again. Then he spoke. The first tactical comment anyone had heard from him in months.

"Hold position. Let them come to us."

Kael started to push forward. Stopped. Considered.

Ren was right. A holding position would let the Voidspawn cluster more densely while advancing through the canyon — less time for them to exploit the high-ground advantage. Higher casualty rate for the creatures, lower risk for the Veilborn.

"Shift defensive stance. Let them come," Kael called. The group repositioned.

The Voidspawn advanced into the prepared line. Ren became a wall of steel. Sera's burst damage devastated the tightly packed creatures. Maren healed from safety. The fight was over in minutes.

After, Kael nodded at Ren. The smallest acknowledgment. But Ren saw it. His silence was choice, not submission. When he did speak, Kael listened.

---

Later that same day, as they were regrouping and Senna adjusted bonds, Sera watched Voss move through his warm-up exercises. She'd been watching him fight for days now. The man had precision, but he was favoring his right side — using it for every heavy strike, every quick parry. His left side was mechanical. Slower.

She waited until his attunement was fresh, then spoke.

"You favor your right side. Your left bond is degraded."

Voss paused. Looked at her. "I know."

"I'll compensate," Sera said. It wasn't a question, wasn't offering sympathy. Just stating a fact. She was good. She could adjust her positioning and her burst angles to cover his weak side, force his opponents into range where his degraded left bond wouldn't matter. "We'll make it work."

Voss nodded. "Good."

Two pragmatists sizing each other up. They understood each other immediately in a way the others didn't — no philosophy, no moral weight, just two competent people establishing working terms. That was all it took.

---

Mira had a nosebleed during a fight. Then another the next day. The Sovereign's proximity made the Veil hum at a frequency that interfered with Veilborn sensitivity — like standing too close to a speaker at full volume. Senna handled it better (decades of experience), and Otho bore it stoically (he was too focused on calculations to notice his own discomfort). But Mira was young, her sensitivity was acute, and the noise hurt.

She didn't complain. She wiped the blood on her sleeve and kept working.

Otho noticed. "The Veil here is less a barrier and more a suggestion," he said. "The Veilborn are absorbing ambient interference."

"I can handle it," Senna said.

"So can I," Mira said.

Otho didn't look reassured.

---

On the seventh day in the Proving Grounds, Kael addressed the full group. All eight of them — five Resonants, three Veilborn — gathered in a cave that was, for the moment, stable.

He explained Otho's theory. The Wellspring. The seal. The plan.

"The Veilborn channel their energy into the Veil at the convergence point. The barrier closes. The Echoes are lost — permanently. The Voidspawn stop spawning. The Sovereign, cut off from its source, weakens and dies." He looked at each person in turn. "The Veilborn lose their powers. They become normal. But they live. Everyone else is saved."

Senna spoke next. "And we do this willingly. No one is executing us. We're choosing."

The group was quiet. Sera looked at the Veilborn. Maren looked at the ground. Ren stood where he always stood — behind Kael, ready.

Voss was the last to speak. "When do we move?"

"Tomorrow."

"Good."

---

The Arbiter of Trials was the Proving Grounds' guardian — an ancient Voidspawn that had absorbed enough of the Veil's energy to develop something like intelligence. It didn't just fight. It *tested*.

In the first phase, it suppressed all element synergy bonuses — stripping the team's elemental advantages and forcing them to fight on archetype power and raw stats alone. Kael felt the loss like a missing limb. The team's damage dropped by a third.

"Archetype synergies only!" Kael called. "Tight formation. Ren, hold center. Sera, burst when you have openings."

In the second phase, the battlefield split — a rift of energy bisecting the arena. The team was forced to fight on two fronts simultaneously. A VIP healer appeared on one side, sustaining the Arbiter's health. Sera's side had the damage to kill it. Maren's side had the healing to survive without it. The division was almost perfectly designed to test whether the team could function as two independent halves.

In the third phase, a countdown. The Arbiter began charging a devastating attack — twenty-five seconds to deal enough damage to interrupt it, or wipe. A pure DPS race on a split battlefield with suppressed synergies.

Voss was magnificent. He threw himself at the Arbiter with everything he had — no conservation, no holding back. Sera matched him, channeling the Crucible's ambient energy despite the corruption cost. Ren absorbed the Arbiter's counterattacks without flinching. Maren kept everyone alive through sheer force of will.

The countdown reached three seconds. Kael landed the killing blow.

The Arbiter dissolved. The Proving Grounds went quiet — the warping, the flickering, the stuttering reality all calming as the entity's influence faded.

Behind them: the path to the Wellspring. Ahead: the Sovereign, filling the sky.

"Together," Kael said. "Whatever happens."

Voss, after a long pause: "Together."
