# Chapter 2 — The Barracks

The recall orders arrived by runner two days after the Warden fell. Military command wanted Kael and Lyric at the Barracks — the central Resonant training ground in the lowlands — to help train new recruits and deal with Voidspawn incursions that had been disrupting exercises. Frontier duty was over.

They left the settlement with handshakes and promises to return. Dren stood at the gate and watched them go.

---

The road south cut through farming country — green, gentle, unremarkable. The kind of landscape that made the Voidspawn threat feel distant. Kael walked point. Lyric handled the conversation. Senna maintained the daily attunement schedule without breaking stride. Otho walked into a fence post.

On the second day, they passed through a village that had been hit.

It wasn't attacked — it was *gone*. The buildings were standing, but the people weren't. Doors hung open. Food sat on tables. A dog wandered between empty houses, confused. Whatever had come through in the night had been fast enough that nobody had time to flee.

Kael swept the perimeter. Clear. Whatever had come through was gone.

Lyric found her in the ruins of the eastern district. A girl — sixteen, maybe — crouched behind a collapsed wall with a broken shovel in her hands. She wasn't hiding. She was watching. Assessing. Her eyes were too steady for someone who'd just lost everything.

Senna recognized what she was immediately. "Veilborn." She approached with her hands up. "It's okay. We're Resonants."

The girl stood. She was tall for her age, lean, with sharp features and sharper eyes. She didn't lower the shovel.

"I know what you are," she said. "I can feel your bonds from here."

Senna looked at Kael. Kael looked at Lyric. Lyric crouched to the girl's eye level.

"What's your name?"

"Mira."

"Are there other survivors, Mira?"

"No Veilborn." A pause. "The others ran east. I stayed because..." She stopped. Started again. "I stayed."

Senna took her. No debate, no discussion. Mira was Veilborn, she was alone, and Senna was Senna. That was enough.

---

On the road to the Barracks, Mira rode on Kael's supply pack when she got tired. He didn't comment on the extra weight. She didn't thank him. They had a mutual understanding: she was there, he was carrying her, and acknowledging it would make it weird.

Lyric tried a joke — something about a Veilborn and an Echo walking into a tavern. It didn't land. He tried another one, worse than the first. Mira's mouth twitched. Not a smile. Close.

"That's terrible," she said.

"Thank you," Lyric said. "I have hundreds more."

Otho started teaching her Veil theory to keep her mind occupied — the mechanics of attunement, the physics of the barrier, the taxonomy of Echoes. He gave her a blank notebook. "For observations," he said. "A good scholar writes everything down."

Mira took the notebook.

---

"Why did you sign up for this?"

Mira asked it on the third morning, walking beside Kael while the others were ahead. No preamble. No context. Teenagers.

"Sign up for what?"

"The military. The fighting. You could've done anything else with what you are."

Kael gave the answer he always gave. "Someone had to."

"That's not a reason. That's an excuse."

He glanced at her. "Fine. Because I'm good at it and the alternative was farming."

"That's worse."

She walked ahead. Kael looked at Lyric, who was watching over his shoulder. Lyric was grinning.

---

The Barracks sprawled across a river valley — training fields, barracks buildings, arenas, an archive. Dozens of Resonant recruits in various stages of training. The place buzzed with organized chaos.

A new Resonant was assigned to their squad — **Ren**, a tank specialist with arms like tree trunks and a vocabulary of roughly twelve words. He communicated through positioning: when Ren stepped in front of you, that was him saying *I've got you*. When he stepped aside, it meant *your turn*.

The training exercises were structured around archetype roles — each drill isolating a specific function. Guardians absorbing rushers. Rangers chipping through tank walls. Sorcerers bursting down enemy healers. The recruits learned their roles. Kael taught through precision. Lyric taught through confidence.

Between exercises, Lyric caught Kael watching Mira practice basic Veilborn attunement with Senna in the training yard. She was clumsy but determined — her fingers didn't know the movements yet, but her instincts were sharp.

"She reminds you of someone," Lyric said.

"Don't."

"I was going to say me. At that age. Annoying, too smart, no idea how much trouble she's in."

"You were worse."

"I was *much* worse."

---

During a live exercise, one of the recruits froze. Ren caught the breach before anyone got hurt, but it was close.

"Pull him," Lyric said afterward.

"Give him another chance," Kael said.

Lyric didn't argue. But he didn't agree.

---

Otho had been spending his evenings in the Barracks archive — a vast stone building filled with pre-Shattering records that few people bothered to read anymore. On the fifth night, he came to the common room practically vibrating, arms full of scrolls.

"You need to see this."

He spread the scrolls across the table. Ancient paper, ancient ink, careful diagrams of Veil energy patterns.

"The archive has pre-Shattering records that I don't think anyone's touched in decades. And there's a reference — oblique, but unmistakable — to a place where Veil energy naturally converges. A fixed point. The scholars called it the *Wellspring*." He pointed at a faded diagram. "They believed it was the oldest and thinnest point in the Veil, where the barrier barely exists at all."

Senna leaned in. A fixed convergence point would explain certain attunement anomalies she'd noticed over the years. Kael asked if it was real.

"Hard to say," Otho hedged. "Fair warning — these texts are charmingly outdated. They thought there were only three elements, not six. They hadn't identified Lightning or Force yet. They called attunement *binding*, as if you were chaining the Echoes rather than harmonizing with them. And they didn't even distinguish between Echoes and Voidspawn — lumped everything under one term, *Veilcrossers*. Just 'things that cross the Veil.' They hadn't worked out that there are two separate planes yet."

He waved dismissively. "But the observational data is solid even if their framework was primitive. They tracked Veil events for generations — locations, frequencies, intensities. And this convergence point keeps appearing."

Mira asked to see the maps. Lyric teased Otho about getting excited over dusty scrolls. The conversation turned to the Wellspring — where it might be, whether it still existed. Kael asked a few questions, then went back to cleaning his gear.

Later, when everyone had gone to bed, Otho was still reading. He'd copied the ancient scholars' Veil-crossing event logs into his own notebook — every recorded instance of something crossing the Veil, plotted by location and frequency.

Senna came to find him for dinner. He closed his notebook. "Coming," he said. "Just finishing up."

---

The Archon appeared on their eighth day at the Barracks — a powerful Voidspawn drawn to the concentration of Resonant energy at the training grounds. It was larger than anything the recruits had faced, and it had a trick: it shifted between combat stances, cycling through different fighting styles like a creature that couldn't decide what it wanted to be.

One moment it was a wall of stone — massive armor, shrugging off attacks. Then it shifted into something predatory, leaping across the field to savage the backline. Then arcane energy crackled from its body in waves, forcing everyone to scatter.

Kael read each stance as it shifted. "It's armoring up — burst it now before the shield regenerates!" Then: "Predator stance — it's diving! Get your healers behind the tanks!" Then: "AoE incoming! Spread formation!"

Lyric kept the recruits steady. Some of them had never seen a creature this large. Ren held the front line like a wall, barely moving, barely speaking, absorbing everything that came his way.

The Archon fell. The recruits erupted.

At the fire afterward, Lyric moved between the groups — checking injuries, trading stories with the recruits, making each cluster feel like they'd been the ones who turned the fight. He had a way of being everywhere at once without seeming to rush.

Kael stood at the edge and watched. Across the flames, Lyric caught his eye. They shared a nod.

The Barracks were safe. The recruits would be fine. The group had grown: Ren was reliable, Mira was learning, Otho had his scrolls.

Orders came the next morning. A contested region in the east — multiple settlements under simultaneous Voidspawn assault, the attacks coordinated in a way that suggested something behind them. They were deploying at full strength.

Kael looked around the camp as they packed. Lyric was helping Mira adjust her pack straps. Ren was already loaded and waiting. Otho was cramming one last scroll into his bag. Senna was humming while she worked.
